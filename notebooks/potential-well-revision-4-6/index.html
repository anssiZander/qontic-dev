Inside an infinite well, V=0. The time-independent Schrödinger equation is:

−ℏ22md2ψdx2=Eψ

The left wall removes the cosine part, leaving ψ(x)=A sin[k(x+L/2)]. The right wall requires sin(kL)=0, so k=nπ/L. The n=0 choice gives ψ=0 everywhere, not a physical normalized state; therefore n=1,2,3,… .

7. Interpretations use the same calculated wavefunction

Changing interpretation changes the meaning assigned to Ψ and measurement, not the energy spectrum. The default state is a superposition so that the pilot-wave example begins with a visible guidance motion.

Interpretation note

Standard / Copenhagen

Ψ is a probability amplitude. The Born rule is included here: |Ψ|² gives the density of possible detection outcomes. The yellow dBB markers are absent because this view does not assign a hidden particle position between measurements.

de Broglie-Bohm / pilot-wave

Ψ guides a definite configuration through v=j/|Ψ|². Yellow markers represent separate possible particle positions across an ensemble of identically prepared one-particle systems, not several particles in one well. In a single real energy eigenstate, j=0 and every marker is stationary; switch back to a superposition to see a nonzero guidance flow.

Many-worlds

Ψ is treated as the physical quantum state. Measurement is described through branching, with |Ψ|² giving branch weights. The calculated well states remain the same.

Ensemble interpretation

Ψ describes repeated preparations. |Ψ|² predicts the distribution seen across many measurements rather than the path of a single system.
